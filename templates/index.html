putCard(declared_card)

The declared_card is the card your opponent claimed to have put. If None, the pile is empty or cards were just drawn — you may put any card. Return a tuple (true_card, declared_card) where true_card is the actual card you play (must be in your hand) and declared_card is what you tell your opponent. If they differ, you are cheating. Return the string "draw" to take 3 cards instead.

checkCard(opponent_declaration)

Decide whether to check your opponent. Return True to check or False to trust their declaration.

getCheckFeedback(...) (optional)

Called at the end of each round with details about who checked and what cards were revealed. Use this to update your strategy between rounds.

Description

During last laboratories we considered prisoner's dilemma as a sequential game. Moreover, we know that players may behave in different ways depending on the number of iterations, the possibility of forgiveness or not the betrayal. We can also considered a game where we can get the knowledge about betrayal only when we want it. The game that we have is a sequential game where each player can choose from the following actions:

  • to betray or not,
  • to check or not.

The pay-off matrix is not constant for the whole game and needs to be updated in each iteration and possible gain or cost may be adjusted individually by each player. Moreover, in each turn each player wants to predict the choice of the opponent.

The game that we are talking about is known as Cheater. We play only part of the card deck: from 9 to ace in 4 colours. Each player at the beginning of the game gets 8 cards. Your task is:

  • put a card on a table which is the same or higher than the card on the top if you have no plan for cheat or
  • put whatever card on a table if you plan to cheat or
  • resign from putting card and just taking 3 cards from the top of the heap.

Remember that you opponent may check if you are saying the truth. Then:

  • if you cheated: you take 3 cards from the top of the heap
  • if you didn't cheat: your opponent take 3 cards from the top of the heap.

Then you switch the roles: your opponent puts cards and you need to decide if you check her/him or not. The game ends when somebody puts all the cards on the table. Try to base on a pay-off matrix that you update after each turn.

Your main task is to write a python script of the player which has the best strategy. Your code should base on the exemplary code which is available in a separate jupyter notebook. Take care especially for the input and output of your player. Moreover, there is a time limit of 0.1 s per decision.

Scoring

Your player needs to go through two stages of scoring:

  1. games with unknown players which implements both more naive and sophisticated approaches and strategies,
  2. games with all people from the group.

Each competition in pairs will be run 100 times.

  • 3 points: your player wins with the naive player,
  • 5 points: your player wins with the player with more advanced strategy.

All scores between will be given basing on your position in the ranking of all players.
